Diluting DNA uses the common dilution formula 'C1 * V1 = C2 * V2' This formula can be explained as: Concentration #1 Multiplied by Volume #1 Equals Concentration #2 Multiplied by Volume #2 For example:

c1 = 10
v1 = 10
c2 = 5

Slide 3

Why does one need to dilute DNA?!?

Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) is a technology that allows microbiologists to accurately read DNA. The process is fast, efficient and becoming cheaper all the time. One of the most common processes performed in the protocol for NGS is the dilution of DNA to an ideal concentration. This is because the chemistry and equipment used during DNA sequencing are highly sensitive and have a narrow window of ideal concentrations that will give an accurate reading of the DNA. This process is often repeated dozens of times during an experiment. Wouldn't it be nice if there was a web application that could compute the exact amount of diluent needed to be added to the stock DNA to achieve the desired concentration? Well now there is!

Why use this app over other apps?

  • This application subtracts out the current volume and gives you only the volume of diluent to be added, saving you from having to do one step of the calculation.

  • This application provides you with the total amount (mass) of DNA present in solution which is a valuable quantity to have for future calculations and publications.
